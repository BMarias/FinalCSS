Brian's Decisions

Some people discover their favorite shows and movies through deep self-reflection, artistic analysis, or years of careful media study.

No scientific research has been done to provide these results.

I, on the other hand, simply stumbled into them like a raccoon finding treasure in a recycling bin.

It all began in middle school, when I first encountered anime—not through some elegant Studio Ghibli masterpiece like most people—but through a chaotic chain of YouTube AMVs set to Linkin Park. I didn’t understand half of what I saw, but there were giant robots, glowing eyes, and someone yelling “Lelouch vi Britannia commands you!” and suddenly my brain said, “Yes. This. This is peak storytelling.” Eventually, I watched Code Geass properly, and it felt like someone took chess, politics, Death Note-level mind games, giant mechs, plot twists, and blended it into the most unhinged emotional rollercoaster imaginable. There’s betrayal, rebellion, questionable decisions, zero-gravity pizza parties—everything required to shape a person’s taste in fiction forever. I finished it staring at my wall like I had just seen the face of God… and He was wearing a Zero mask.

Years later, my cinematic soulmate arrived: Dune: Part Two. A movie so dramatic that even the sand has character development. I walked into the theater expecting a sci-fi movie about sand and spicy space politics. I walked out feeling like I had witnessed a prophecy, experienced a spiritual awakening, and now belonged to a desert warrior tribe. The visuals, the soundtrack, the worms the size of apartment complexes—perfection. Hans Zimmer basically slapped my soul with a soundwave and I thanked him. Between Code Geass and Dune: Part Two, I realized I apparently love stories where a brilliant, morally-gray protagonist gains way too much power, challenges empires, rides something dramatic (mechs… worms… same energy), and leaves me questioning my life choices by the ending.

So yes—after years of watching more anime and movies than I’d like to admit, I can confidently say that Lelouch Lamperouge and Paul Atreides have permanently burned themselves into my “Favorite Characters I Should Not Be Rooting For” list. And honestly? I wouldn’t have it any other way.
